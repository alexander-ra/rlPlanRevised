## 🏆 The High-Value Targets (The "Do This" List)

### 1. B2B Enterprise Fraud & AML Engine (The Neo-Bank Play)
* **The Concept:** London is the fintech capital of Europe (Revolut, Monzo, Wise). They are bleeding billions to synthetic identity fraud and adversarial AI attacks. You build a dynamic MARL/ML anomaly detection engine that adapts to new fraud vectors faster than static rules.
* **Your Role:** Build the ML pipeline, the data ingestion architecture, and the beautiful observability dashboard that their compliance officers will use daily. 
* **His Role:** Use the Oxford/Finance network to bypass lower-level engineering managers and pitch directly to Chief Risk Officers (CROs) at mid-tier European fintechs.
* **💰 Estimated Payoff:** B2B Enterprise SaaS. Contracts range from €100k - €300k+ ARR per client. Secure 4 clients, and you have a €1M ARR business with 90% gross margins. 

### 2. The Niche Private Credit / Deal Sourcing AI (The Private Equity Play)
* **The Concept:** Private Equity (PE) and Private Credit funds waste thousands of human hours reading messy financial documents to find acquisition targets. You build an "Agentic Workflow" (Multi-Agent LLMs + RL) that crawls, ingests, and scores thousands of mid-market private companies based on the fund's specific thesis.
* **Your Role:** Stitch together LLMs and RL agents to automate financial data extraction and company scoring. Build the UX that allows PE associates to interact with the data.
* **His Role:** He already knows these PE guys. He sells them the platform as a way to fire half their junior analysts and double their deal flow.
* **💰 Estimated Payoff:** €10,000 - €25,000 / month per PE firm. Highly sticky revenue. 

### 3. Automated Treasury Hedging for Mid-Market Corporates
* **The Concept:** Massive corporations have entire teams to hedge against currency (FX) or commodity price swings. Mid-market companies (€50M-€500M revenue) do not. You build an RL agent that analyzes a company's cash flow and recommends optimal hedging strategies.
* **Your Role:** Build the pricing models and the reinforcement learning agents that simulate market volatility to recommend trades.
* **His Role:** Sells the software to corporate CFOs who want institutional-grade risk management without hiring a $500k/year quant team.
* **💰 Estimated Payoff:** €5,000 - €15,000 / month SaaS subscription per company. 

### 4. The Boutique MEV / DeFi Quant Fund (The Web3 Cash Cow)
* **The Concept:** You bypass TradFi entirely and go into crypto. You use your MARL exploitation background to build arbitrage and MEV (Maximum Extractable Value) bots. 
* **Your Role:** Pure execution. Write the low-latency Rust/C++ code and the mathematical models to exploit decentralized exchanges.
* **His Role:** Raises the initial €2M–€5M capital pool from high-net-worth Oxford alumni or London family offices looking for high-risk/high-yield crypto exposure. He handles the horrific legal and compliance structuring required to run a crypto fund.
* **💰 Estimated Payoff:** Standard 2% management fee / 20% performance fee. If you run €5M and generate a 30% return, the fund clears €400k in fees year one, scaling infinitely with AUM.

### 5. AI-Driven Alternative Data Brokerage
* **The Concept:** Hedge funds are desperate for "Alpha" (an edge). You build ML scrapers that gather obscure alternative data (e.g., European supply chain logistics, localized sentiment, obscure shipping manifests) and use ML to clean and structure it into actionable trading signals.
* **Your Role:** Data engineering, scraping, ML structuring, and API creation.
* **His Role:** Takes the data feeds to London hedge funds and sells them annual data subscriptions.
* **💰 Estimated Payoff:** Data feeds sell for €50k - €150k+ annually per fund.

## 🚫 The Traps (The "Sounds Good, But Avoid" List)

### 6. The B2C Retail "Robo-Advisor" App
* **The Concept:** "Let's build an AI app that helps regular people invest their savings!"
* **Why it’s a Trap:** Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) will bleed you to death. Retail investors are annoying, they have very little money, and they panic-withdraw. Furthermore, the regulatory burden of managing retail money in the UK/EU is catastrophic. You will spend all your money on lawyers, not compute.
* **💰 Financial Reality:** You charge users €5/month and spend €50 in Facebook ads just to acquire them. Guaranteed bankruptcy. Leave retail to Vanguard.

### 7. High-Frequency Trading (HFT) Prop Shop
* **The Concept:** "We will build RL bots to day-trade the S&P 500 and beat Jane Street!"
* **Why it’s a Trap:** You are bringing a knife to a nuclear war. HFT is no longer about math; it is about hardware. Firms spend hundreds of millions laying private fiber-optic cables to shave nanoseconds off execution times. Deep RL is currently far too slow to compute at that timescale anyway.
* **💰 Financial Reality:** You will lose all your capital to transaction fees, latency arbitrage, and hardware costs. 

### 8. The Generic "AI Consulting" Agency
* **The Concept:** "We will just be an agency and build whatever AI tools companies ask us for."
* **Why it’s a Trap:** Unscalable and exhausting. Your Oxford MBA partner will spend his life pitching €10k chatbot projects to confused middle managers. You will be essentially running an outsourced dev shop, competing with cheap labor globally.
* **💰 Financial Reality:** Capped by billable hours. High stress, low margins, zero equity upside.
