### B. Scientific Research and Pedagogical Work

**1. Problem Scope:**  
Modern online card game AIs like Libratus, DeepStack, and Pluribus show that computers can surpass humans in complex game formats. They use strategies close to Game-Theory-Optimal (GTO). However, these systems rarely adapt dynamically to a specific opponent and are not designed for fraud/collusion detection. Other available solutions — scripted bots, simplified CFR agents, or basic RL experiments — cover separate aspects but are usually either too static or too easy to exploit. This leaves a gap for a methodology that combines stability, adaptability, and mechanisms for fair play.

**2. Topic of the Dissertation:**  
Research on the possibilities for applying Artificial Intelligence in computer games.

**3. Rationale for the Topic:**  
The idea of the dissertation is to study and explore existing AI systems for developing adaptive games.  

The goal is to develop an adaptive AI agent to be used in various computer games. The agent will start from a safe strategy but will be able to improve its outcome against sub-optimal opponents. This will remove the limitations of previous systems, which rarely dynamically adapt their behavior.  

To achieve this goal, the following main tasks must be solved:  
- Research existing solutions for using adaptive AI in computer games;  
- Design an AI agent capable of adapting to different players, as well as detecting fraud attempts;  
- Select appropriate methods and tools and develop a simulation environment with an interface for connecting various agents;  
- Create a variety of opponents;  
- Develop an adaptive AI agent;  
- Train the agent against different opponents;  
- Implement and test the system.  

The developed system will be heavily tested with pilot users, and the obtained results will be analyzed.
